Back cover

Bonjour Tristesse tells the story of Cécile, who leads a carefree life with her widowed father and his young mistress until, one hot summer on the Riviera, he decides to remarry - with devastating consequences.

Opening

This strange new feeling of mine, obsessing me by its sweet languor, is such that I am reluctant to dignify it with the fine, solemn name of 'sadness'. It is a feeling so self-indulgent and complete in itself that I am almost ashamed of it, whereas I had always looked upon sadness as being a worthy emotion. Before, I did not know what sadness was, though I knew wat it was to be languorous, to have regrets and, more rarely, to feel remorse. Today it is as if I am enfolded in some silken thing, soft and enervating that sets me apart from others.
